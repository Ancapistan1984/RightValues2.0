Ideologies:

Nazism: Nazism is a form of Fascism that is a bit more extreme, and values the race of people more. It favours a centralized government. The ideology was founded in the 1920s by the Nazi Party, which got popular during 1933 in the Third Reich. The flag includes a swastika, an iconic symbol for Nazism.

Fascism: Fascism is an ideology that favours a centralized government. Fascists think a nation's power should be put in a high importance, and also think many things, including most property, should be dependent on the government. They believe on strong trust and dependency to the state. Fascism is founded in Italy in 1915.

Monarchism: Monarchism is a ideology that advocates a large government with a dictatorship, which the leadership is passed on by genetics. It is the most popular ideology for millenia. Exisiting monarchies include the Vatican, as well as Islamic monarchs like Saudi Arabia or Yemen.

Feudalism: Feudalism is an ideology that is based on Monarchism. It is basically a monarchy with high importance of capitalism. Higher classes rule over lower classes' property in exchange for land.

Constitutional Monarchism: Constitutional Monarchism is a form of Monarchism where the leader has a lot of power, but needs to follow a constitution to rule.

Conservatism: Conservatism is an ideology that is mainly about preserving the western political model. They want a small government, large military spending and low taxes. It is one of the most popular right-wing ideologies in the West.

Liberalism: Liberalism is an left-leaning ideology that advocates for policies like state ownership. They don't support socialism.

Neo-Conservatism: Neo-Conservatism is a new form of Conservatism. Neo-Conservatives think that the goal of western nations is to bring their model to the world. They are more internationally focused.

Libertarianism: Libertarians are generally similar to conservatives culturally, but want a much smaller state/government.

Individualist Anarchism: Individualist Anarchism is a form of anarchism which strongly advocates individualism, alongside with an anarchist government.

Agorism: Agorists think that people could live in harmony without a state in an anarchist society, called the non-agression principle. They favour a free market.

Anarcho-Capitalism: Anarcho-Capitalism is an anarchist ideology that wants big cooperations to rule over society. It is an extreme form of anarchy and capitalism.

Minarchism: Minarchism is an ideology that wants a very small government. They think the purpose of the government is only for protecting and enforcing laws. This type of government is called the Night-Watchman State.

Technocracy: Technocracy is a ideology that promotes the idea of people who are experts of technology should rule the nation.

State Capitalism: State Capitalism is basically a authotarian left-wing form of Capitalism. It is culturally left wing but economically right wing. China adopts this ideology.

Neo-Liberalism: Neo-Liberalism is a culturally liberal but economically capitalist ideology. Many people consider this as the current status quo.
